The Structure
Hey [name], Saad here — appreciate you getting back to me about connecting [your company] with [type of partner].
Before we jump on a call, wanted to give you a bit of context on how this works, because it’s usually not what people expect. The way we operate is closer to how referrals already happen — just systematized.
What that means in practice is that we don’t start with outreach or introductions. We start upstream, by slowing everything down and getting extremely clear on who actually makes sense to connect in the first place.
We spend most of the time being very precise about who the right partner actually is. Not titles, but people with the exact [pain point / risk–upside profile that would be interested]. Senior enough to act, reputation-driven, and with enough autonomy to move things forward without any friction.
Once that’s clear, there’s no pitching. We treat it as a relevance check. The message is never “here’s a company you should talk to.” It’s closer to: “We’re seeing a small number of [type of companies] exploring [specific move] because of [specific upside]. Your [team / lab / group] came up as one that might actually care. If this is irrelevant, we drop it.”
Everything is pre-filtered on our side of course. If there’s curiosity, we make a clean intro. If there isn’t, we don’t push. So think of this less as replacing your existing referrals — assuming you already have them — and more as extending that same circle slightly. Reaching the same kind of people who would say yes for the same reasons, but who just aren’t in your immediate orbit.
This usually results in a small number of very deliberate conversations, not volume. If someone wanted scale for its own sake, I’d tell them this isn’t a fit. If that framing resonates, we can do a short call to see whether this is worth taking any further.
