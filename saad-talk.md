# Talk Setup (Cal.com)

Alright, now that you have your VSL, prospects need somewhere to book.

This is where Cal.com come in. I use Cal.com because it's free, clean,
and does everything you need without the bloat.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 1) Cal.com setup

**Call Name (Title)**\
Connector Alignment Call\
(15 minutes)

*alt options if you want:* - Partner Alignment Call\
- Intro Feasibility Call\
- Connector Review

**Description (Above The Calendar)**

This is not a sales call.\
The purpose is to determine whether it makes sense to route
introductions in your situation.\
If there's no clear fit, we'll know quickly and end the call early.

**Duration**\
15--20 minutes\
(never 30 --- premium = short)

**Questions (THIS IS THE CORE)**

1.  What outcome are you trying to create through partnerships or
    introductions?\
2.  Who do you believe the right partner is --- not by title, but by
    function?\
3.  Why does this matter now and not later?\
4.  What would make an introduction irrelevant for you?\
5.  How do you currently handle referrals or warm intros, if at all?\
6.  Anything I should know before we speak? (Optional)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2) Pre-Call Hacks

**Limit booking windows**

If you let people book 2--3 weeks out, the close rate drops 80%.

Force 48--72h windows → increases perceived importance.

Only allow booking 24--48h forward.

**Over-Communicate Before The Call**

Automation sends: - Confirmation email\
- 24-hour reminder\
- 2-hour reminder\
- 5-min reminder

## 3) What Happens After They Book

Send a Partner Map before the call.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## What is a Partner Map?

A 1-page doc that shows: - 3--5 partner types\
- What they need\
- Why timing matters\
- Which to prioritize

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Partner Map Template

\[Company Name\] --- Partner Map

Prepared for: \[Name, Title\]\
Prepared by: \[Your Name\]\
Date: \[Date\]

Context:\
Short analysis + 3--5 partner types.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Partner Types

Each includes: - What they are\
- What they need\
- Why timing matters\
- Example companies

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## My Recommendation

Pick ONE partner type and explain why.
