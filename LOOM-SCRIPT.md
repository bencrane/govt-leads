# Loom Script — Bullseye Demo

## The Rules

- This is NOT a product demo. You're sharing your workflow.
- Casual, confident. Like a screen recording you fired off in 90 seconds.
- Never say "I built this." Never say "this is my platform."
- The app should already be on screen when you start talking.
- Don't narrate every field or feature. Let the viewer absorb it.

---

## Beat 1: The Opening (5-10 seconds)

Bullseye is already on screen. Signal cards, chart, data everywhere. You're mid-workflow.

> "So what you're looking at here is Bullseye — this is basically how I track what's happening across the market. Contract wins, hiring surges, expansion signals... I pull it all into one place so I can see who's growing before they even start posting jobs."

Don't dwell. Move on.

---

## Beat 2: The Market Insight (10-15 seconds)

Gesture at the chart area. Deliver a market insight casually, like you're briefing a colleague.

> "One thing we're seeing right now is defense spending is up significantly — almost doubled in the last 12 months. That's a lot of companies about to need skilled labor."

Then reference the cards below: "For example, right now there are companies in the Southeast that just won contracts and are already posting for welders, machinists, shipfitters..."

You're not explaining the tool. You're sharing what you see.

---

## Beat 3: The "How We'd Work Together" Bridge (15-20 seconds)

Transition from "here's what I see" to "here's what it looks like for you."

> "When we work with a partner, we align on the roles you actually place, which industries, which regions — so for example, if you're doing industrial staffing in the Midwest..."

This is your Cmd+K moment. Open the command palette, type something specific (e.g., "midwest manufacturing"). Results flood in. Select a list. The page reshapes to show a filtered intelligence view.

> "...we'd build something like this. These are all companies actively hiring in your vertical, right now."

---

## Beat 4: The Dossier Flash (5-10 seconds)

Click one company from the list. The profile slides in. Don't walk through every field.

> "...and you can see they're already posting for welders, headcount's up 18% year over year, just won a $4M DoD contract..."

Close it. Move on. The viewer saw the depth.

---

## Beat 5: The Close (5-10 seconds)

Wrap quickly. The mystery tabs (Trends, Market Intel, Watchlists) are visible but you never touched them. The viewer's imagination fills in something better than anything you could show.

> "So that's the idea — if this is interesting, let's find 15 minutes to talk about what it would look like for you guys specifically."

---

## Key Phrases That Do the Work

- **"how I track"** — personal, it's a workflow, not a product
- **"before they even start posting jobs"** — the value prop, dropped casually
- **"one thing we're seeing"** — analyst energy, not sales energy
- **"when we work with a partner, we align on..."** — implies a real process without overselling
- **"for example"** — makes everything feel live and specific

## What NOT to Say

- "Let me show you this cool thing"
- "I built this platform"
- "As you can see here..."
- "This is our proprietary technology"
- Anything that sounds like a feature walkthrough
- Don't explain what the tabs do
- Don't apologize for anything on screen
